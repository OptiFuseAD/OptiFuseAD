OptiFuseAD: Intent-Driven Cross-Modal Fusion with Physically-Grounded Refinement for Robust Autonomous Driving

Ran Bu1, Tong Lu1, Junjie Zhang1, Yang Shuo1, Jun Ye1, Yanzi Miao1,
1China University of Mining and Technology,
Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

Information fusion is pivotal for robust End-to-End Autonomous Driving (E2EAD), yet current vision-centric approaches primarily focus on geometric fusion, often neglecting the integration of high-level cognitive reasoning. This limitation creates a semantic gap: while models can reconstruct spatial details, they struggle to fuse causal logic into motion planning for complex interactions. To address the heterogeneity between dense sensory data and sparse semantic knowledge, we propose OptiFuseAD, a hierarchical cross-modal fusion framework. First, we introduce a Cross-Modal Knowledge Internalization mechanism. By employing a Teacher-Student distillation paradigm, we fuse the reasoning capabilities of Vision Language Models (VLMs) directly into the visual backbone. This establishes a cognitive reflex that aligns real-time visual latent spaces with human-like reasoning manifolds, effectively compensating for the absence of explicit depth sensors. Second, we design an Intent-Driven Modulation strategy, where internalized semantic intent acts as a cognitive query to selectively fuse task-relevant spatiotemporal features, ensuring precise alignment between cognition and perception. Finally, to mitigate the probabilistic uncertainty of generative models, a Physical Knowledge Fusion module serves as a deterministic arbiter at the decision level, refining trajectories to strictly adhere to kinematic safety constraints. Extensive experiments on nuScenes and NAVSIM demonstrate that OptiFuseAD outperforms state-of-the-art multi-sensor baselines, achieving superior planning performance with real-time inference efficiency, proving that cognitive-enhanced fusion is a viable alternative to hardware-heavy sensor fusion.
